Geometric topology

The Poincaré Conjecture

In dimension three, simple connectivity completely determines the topology of a closed manifold.

Conjecture

Let $M$ be a closedCompact and without boundary: $M$ is compact and $\partial M=\varnothing$., connected$M$ has one connected component; for a manifold, this is equivalent to path-connectedness. topological $3$-manifoldA Hausdorff, second-countable space locally homeomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^3$.. If $\pi_1(M)=0$For a basepoint $x\in M$, $\pi_1(M,x)$ is the group of based loops modulo homotopy. Triviality means every loop is null-homotopic., then $M\cong S^3$Here $\cong$ denotes homeomorphism and $S^3=\{x\in\mathbb{R}^4:\lVert x\rVert=1\}$..

Perelman proves this classification by evolving a Riemannian metric under Ricci flow, resolving singularities by surgery, and recovering the topology from finite-time extinction.

Henri Poincaré posed the problem in 1904 while laying the foundations of three-dimensional topology. In 1982, Richard Hamilton introduced Ricci flow and proposed using it to deform a manifold toward a geometrically understandable form. Grigori Perelman's 2002–2003 preprints supplied the decisive ideas, entropy, noncollapsing, and control of singularities, needed to complete Hamilton's program. The detailed account by Morgan and Tian is the principal proof track followed here.

Formalization roadmap

The proof architecture

The formal development follows the analytic and topological dependencies in Perelman's argument.

1Control the flow and its singularities
Ricci flowThe metric evolves by $\partial_t g_{ij} = -2R_{ij}$; the maximum principle bounds curvature and Hamilton compactness takes limits of rescalings.κ-noncollapsingPerelman's reduced volume is monotone along the flow, forcing $\operatorname{Vol} B(x,r) \ge \kappa\, r^3$ where $|\mathrm{Rm}| \le r^{-2}$ — the flow never collapses.κ-solutionsBlowing up at a singularity gives an ancient, $\kappa$-noncollapsed, nonnegatively-curved model.
2Model the singularities, then cut them out
Canonical neighborhoodsEvery high-curvature point lies in a neck $\approx S^2 \times \mathbb{R}$ or a cap — a short, fixed list of local models.Ricci flow with surgeryCut along $\delta$-necks, glue in capped standard solutions, and restart; the parameters keep the flow $\kappa$-noncollapsed and canonical.Long-time existenceSurgery times do not accumulate, so the flow with surgery runs for all $t \ge 0$.
3Recover the topology under $\pi_1(M)=0$
Finite-time extinctionA min-max width associated to nontrivial $\pi_2$ or $\pi_3$ decreases under the flow and forces extinction in finite time.Surgery topologyTracking neck surgeries and discarded components yields a connected-sum decomposition into spherical space forms and $S^2$-bundles over $S^1$.
$M \cong S^3$
Van Kampen turns connected sums into free products of fundamental groups. Trivial $\pi_1(M)$ excludes the nonspherical summands and nontrivial spherical space forms.
