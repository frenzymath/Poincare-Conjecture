This project

How this project works

This project is an open Lean 4 formalization of Perelman's proof — and anyone is welcome to contribute.

To tackle a formalization of this size, we gathered a library of reference books — across Riemannian geometry, PDE, and algebraic topology — that we formalize source-faithfully alongside the conjecture itself.

The development currently relies on Archon Horizon, an agentic formalization framework, complemented by human review and direct contributions to both the Lean code and the blueprints.

Geometric topology

The Poincaré Conjecture

In dimension three, simple connectivity completely determines the topology of a closed manifold.

Conjecture

Let $M$ be a closedCompact and without boundary: $M$ is compact and $\partial M=\varnothing$., connected$M$ has one connected component; for a manifold, this is equivalent to path-connectedness. topological $3$-manifoldA Hausdorff, second-countable space locally homeomorphic to $\mathbb{R}^3$.. If $\pi_1(M)=0$For a basepoint $x\in M$, $\pi_1(M,x)$ is the group of based loops modulo homotopy. Triviality means every loop is null-homotopic., then $M\cong S^3$Here $\cong$ denotes homeomorphism and $S^3=\{x\in\mathbb{R}^4:\lVert x\rVert=1\}$..

Background

Henri Poincaré posed the problem in 1904, at the origin of three-dimensional topology. Richard Hamilton introduced Ricci flow in 1982 as a way to deform a manifold toward a recognizable geometry, and Grigori Perelman's 2002–2003 preprints supplied the decisive ideas that completed the program. This formalization follows the detailed account of Morgan and Tian.

Formalization roadmap

The proof architecture

Perelman proves it by evolving a Riemannian metric under Ricci flow, resolving singularities by surgery, and recovering the topology from finite-time extinction — the three stages below.

1
Control the flow and its singularities
Ricci flow
The metric evolves by $\partial_t g_{ij} = -2R_{ij}$; the maximum principle bounds curvature and Hamilton compactness takes limits of rescalings.
κ-noncollapsing
Perelman's reduced volume is monotone along the flow, forcing $\operatorname{Vol} B(x,r) \ge \kappa\, r^3$ where $|\mathrm{Rm}| \le r^{-2}$ — the flow never collapses.
κ-solutions
Blowing up at a singularity gives an ancient, $\kappa$-noncollapsed, nonnegatively-curved model.
2
Model the singularities, then cut them out
Canonical neighborhoods
Every high-curvature point lies in a neck $\approx S^2 \times \mathbb{R}$ or a cap — a short, fixed list of local models.
Ricci flow with surgery
Cut along $\delta$-necks, glue in capped standard solutions, and restart; the parameters keep the flow $\kappa$-noncollapsed and canonical.
Long-time existence
Surgery times do not accumulate, so the flow with surgery runs for all $t \ge 0$.
3
Recover the topology under $\pi_1(M)=0$
Finite-time extinction
A min-max width associated to nontrivial $\pi_2$ or $\pi_3$ decreases under the flow and forces extinction in finite time.
Surgery topology
Tracking neck surgeries and discarded components yields a connected-sum decomposition into spherical space forms and $S^2$-bundles over $S^1$.
✓
$M \cong S^3$
Van Kampen turns connected sums into free products of fundamental groups. Trivial $\pi_1(M)$ excludes the nonspherical summands and nontrivial spherical space forms.
