\chapter*{About This Blueprint}

This project follows Peter Petersen, \emph{Riemannian Geometry}, third edition,
Graduate Texts in Mathematics 171 (Springer, 2016) \cite{petersen2016}. The
electronic edition is available through
\href{https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-26654-1}{doi}.
A PDF version is also available
\href{https://ebooks.karbust.me/Mathematics/Peter\%20Petersen\%20-\%20Riemannian\%20Geometry\%20(1998)\%20[978-1-4757-6434-5].pdf}{here}.

Petersen develops the Riemannian geometry used throughout the Poincare
project: metrics and connections, curvature, geodesics and distance,
sectional- and Ricci-curvature comparison, Killing fields, symmetric spaces,
holonomy, and geometric convergence. These results provide geometric and
topological tools used in three-manifold theory and Ricci-flow arguments; the
book is background for the formalization rather than a standalone proof of the
Poincare conjecture.

The blueprint is a concise, independently worded distillation, not a verbatim
copy of the book. It retains the mathematical statements and proof ideas
needed for formalization. \textbf{We recommend reading the blueprint alongside
the book.} The \emph{dcref} tag records the corresponding theorem, definition,
or other source number in Petersen's book and is the intended guide for that
comparison.

The blueprint covers all twelve chapters of the book: Riemannian metrics,
derivatives, curvature, geometric examples, geodesics and distance,
sectional-curvature comparison, Ricci-curvature comparison, Killing fields,
the Bochner technique, symmetric spaces and holonomy, convergence, and global
sectional-curvature comparison.
