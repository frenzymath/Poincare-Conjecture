\chapter*{About This Blueprint}

This project follows John M. Lee, \emph{Introduction to Riemannian Manifolds},
second edition, Graduate Texts in Mathematics 176 (Springer, 2018)
\cite{Lee2018Riemannian}. The electronic edition is available through
\href{https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-91755-9}{doi}.
A PDF version is also available
\href{https://lps.elte.hu/gomori/spacetime-geometry/readings/\%28Graduate\%20texts\%20in\%20mathematics\%20176\%29\%20Lee\%2C\%20John\%20M\%20-\%20Introduction\%20to\%20Riemannian\%20manifolds\%20\%28Corrected\%20version\%20of\%20second\%20edition\%29-Springer\%20Nature\%20\%282018\%29.pdf}{here}.

Lee develops the Riemannian geometry used throughout the Poincare project:
metrics, affine and Levi-Civita connections, geodesics and distance, curvature,
Riemannian submanifolds, the Gauss--Bonnet theorem, Jacobi fields, comparison
theory, and relations between curvature and topology. These results supply
foundational geometric and topological tools for three-manifold theory and
Ricci-flow arguments; the book supports the formalization rather than proving
the Poincare conjecture by itself.

The blueprint is a concise, independently worded mathematical distillation,
not a transcription of the book. It retains the statements and proof ideas
needed for formalization. \textbf{We recommend reading the blueprint alongside
the book.} The \emph{dcref} tag identifies the corresponding theorem,
definition, problem, or other numbered item in Lee's text and is the intended
guide for comparison with the source.

The blueprint covers all twelve chapters: the meaning of curvature, Riemannian
metrics, model manifolds, connections, the Levi-Civita connection, geodesics
and distance, curvature, Riemannian submanifolds, Gauss--Bonnet, Jacobi fields,
comparison theory, and curvature and topology. It also includes the three
review appendices on smooth manifolds, tensors, and Lie groups.
